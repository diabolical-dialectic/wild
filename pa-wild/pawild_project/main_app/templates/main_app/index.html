Guided Trecking:

Black Forest Trail: (Difficult)

Located in the Tiadaghton State Forest, the Black Forest Trail and it's many side trails reside in Lycoming and Potter counties. The Black Forest Trail is a loop of a little over 43 miles with the trailhead proper being just north of Slate Run, PA. The Black Forest Trail gets its name from the dense hemlocks that once grew here, keeping the forest floor in an everlasting darkness. The trail is challenging with many steep ascents and descents. However, the vistas make it worth the effort.

Loyalsock Trail: (Moderate)

The Loyalsock Trail is a rugged wilderness backpacking trail. One segment of the LT follows the Native American Towanda Path which predates European settlement. The trail visits a vast array of highlights of the Loyalsock State Forest including, scenic vistas and cascading waterfalls.

Pinchot Trail: (Easy)

The Pinchot Trail is located in the Lakawana State Forest. On this hike you will experience Blueberry bogs, swamps and healthy hardwood forest with thick understory of Mountain Laurel and Rhododendron.

We offer a variety of Guided Trecking packages and can accomodate any size group and skill level.

Loyalsock's stunning in-stream geology of river rocks and big boulders make this an awesome experience and prime Tenkara territory.

Clark Creek:

This beautiful wild stretch of water twenty minutes outside of Harrisburg, PA is an underrated gem. The stream is the water that flows out of the Dehart dam and boasts a large population of wild brown trout and many wild brook.

Slate Run:

Slate Run is one of Pennsylvania's few freestone streams and is prime Tenkara water located in the rugged, mountainous setting along the Black Forest Trail. Wild brook trout and a few brown trout reside in the upper part of the stream and wild brown trout make their home in the lower section. While most anglers prefer the brown trout fishing in the lower section we use our Tenkara skills to conquer the uppper.

Bushcraft Lessons:

Fire:

Our classes will provide you with simple repeatable simple steps to make sure you will never struggle to get a fire going again. There is nothing worse after a long day in the backcountry than getting to camp and facing a long battle to get your fire started, especially when you are cold and hungry! Your PA-Wild guides will teach you our six simple steps to ensure you have your campfire blazing in no time.

Shelter:

In this course you will learn how to build 5 different bushcraft shelters that you can craft using natural resources that can be found in the woods. It's important to be able to build shelters out of natural resources so that you will be able to survive in the woods even if you have a lack of man-made materials.

Navigation:

Navigation is a foundational bushcraft skill around which all other bushcraft skills should be built. The skill to navigate effectivly in teh outdoors is an absolutely key if you plan to venture into the backcountry and its importance grows in direct proportion to the remoteness of the environment.
